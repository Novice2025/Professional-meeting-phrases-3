Scenario 1: The New Project Proposal

You are presenting a new project idea. You want to focus on easy wins first, explain that the idea is already in development, and then propose a detailed analysis to get more information.

  • To suggest focusing on easy wins: "We should start with the **low-hanging fruit** to build momentum quickly."
  • To state that the project is in development: "This project is currently **in the pipeline** and will be ready next quarter."
  • To propose a detailed analysis: "We need to schedule a **deep dive** to explore all the potential risks."

Scenario 2: The Team Strategy Meeting

Your team is discussing a difficult issue. You want to propose using a new tool to make a process more efficient, and then you want to postpone the rest of the discussion until a later meeting.

  • To propose using a resource: "I think we can **leverage** the new software to **streamline** our workflow."
  • To suggest postponing a topic: "That's a complex topic. Let's **put a pin in it** and come back to it next week."
  • To inform a colleague you are aware of an issue: "I know there's a problem with the new system, it's definitely **on my radar**."

Scenario 3: The Catch-Up with a Colleague

You're talking with a colleague about the company's future. You want to talk about how a new technology has changed the industry and remind them that the company's main strength is its people.

  • To describe a major change: "The transition to remote work has created a **paradigm shift** in how we collaborate."
  • To describe a main strength: "Our ability to adapt quickly is our true **core competency**."
  • To ask for a quick contact: "Feel free to **ping me** if you have any updates on the project."
